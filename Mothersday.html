1854 US Gold Dollar - Type 1 Liberty Head
Designer: James B. Longacre | Composition: .900 Gold & .100 Copper | Weight: 1.67g | Diameter: 13mm | Mintage 855,502

“Gold Served as money or established the monetary value of currencies longer than any other material. The use of gold coins was widespread in Europe by 300 B.C.

In what became the United States, the earliest coins circulated were foreign, mostly silver and gold, brought from Europe, as well as made in Spanish possessions in the New World. The Coinage Act in 1792 established an independent monetary system with the dollar
as the basic U.S. monetary unit containing 24.75 grains of fine gold, based on a world price of $19.39 a troy ounce (480 grains). Congress changed the gold specification in 1834 and again in 1837, when it set the dollar price of gold at $20.67 an ounce.

In 1933, U.S. citizens were prohibited from holding monetary gold in the United States; this was extended in 1961 to gold held abroad as well. The dollar price was set at $35 per ounce in 1934. Use of gold in international trade was further restricted as the price rose.
The government revalued it at $38 per ounce in 1972, then $42.22 in 1973. The price has fluctuated widely in recent years. All restrictions on holding gold were removed on December 31, 1974.
Current Gold price May 9, 2024 is $2359.37 per troy ounce.

Coinage of the gold dollar was authorized by the act of March 3, 1849. The weight was 25.8 grains, 0.900 fineness. The first type, struck until 1854, is known as the Liberty Head or small sized Type 1.” - The Official Red Book
