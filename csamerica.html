1 Peso

Composition: Silver

Edge: Lettered - INDEPENDENCIA Y LIBERTAD

This coin features José Morelos - born 1765, died 1815. José was an ordained Catholic priest, and one of the greatest military leaders in the Mexican War of Independence. He joined the insurgents in 1810 and led major victories during the war, capturing many Spanish possessions.

In 1813, Morelos presented a document entitled Sentimientos de la Nación (Sentiments of the Nation), formally declaring Mexican independence from Spain. The document also abolished slavery and eliminated racial division, naming all native-born individuals "American".
In late 1813, José Morelos started his fourth military campaign. In 1815 he suffered a defeat, and was captured. The church had jurisdiction for his imprisonment and trial since he was a Catholic priest. He was charged with treason, disloyalty to the crown, and was sentenced to death. Morelos was executed by firing squad on 22 December 1815, north of Mexico City. José Morelos is considered a national hero of Mexico.

Mint mark: o over M - Mexico City Mint (Casa de Moneda de México). The Casa de Moneda was established on May 11 1535 by Spanish decree, creating the first mint in the Americas. The mint was built on the remains of Casa Denegrida de Moctezuma (The Black House of Moctezuma). The Black House was a room in the Royal Palace of Aztec Emperor Moctezuma II. It had a black basalt floor and was where Moctezuma would meditate.
