Mexico

1 Peso 1871, Mexico
Composition: Silver .9027 | Weight: 27.01g | Diameter: 37.3mm | Thickness: 2.9mm
Mint Mark: Mo - Mexico City, Mexico (1535-date)



Mexico City Mint
La Casa de Moneda de México is the oldest mint in the Americas. It was established on 11 May 1535 by a decree from the Spanish Crown to create the first mint in the Americas.
It was built on top of the remains of The Casa Denegrida de Moctezuma (The Black House of Moctezuma). This was a room within the royal palace where Moctezuma II would meditate (The ninth Emperor of the Aztec Empire, 1502-1520). It was a windowless room, fully painted black.

The second Franco-Mexican War was a military invasion of the Republic of Mexico by the French Empire. Lasting from 1861 - 1867, when France abandoned the war. The Mexican Republic tried Emperor Maximilian (Austrian Archduke and Emperor of the Second Mexican Empire) for aiding the French invasion of Mexico and attempting to overthrow the Mexican government. He was executed by firing squad on June 19, 1867.

Benito Juárez was the 26th President of Mexico, from 1858 - 1871. He was of Zapotec ancestry, the first and only indigenous president of Mexico.
