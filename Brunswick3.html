16 Gute Groschen
Brunswick-Lüneburg-Celle
1694
Composition: Silver
Weight: 16.89g
Diameter: 35.7mm
Thickness: 1.9mm


George William, Duke of Brunswick
House: Hanover
Born 1624 - Died 1705


This coin features the horse motif, the Saxon Steed. This symbol was adopted by Brunswick and House Hanover. Above the horse reads "QUO FAS ET GLORIA DUCUNT" which translates to "Where Justice and Glory Lead" or "Wherever Right and Glory Lead".
The value on the coin is listed as "XVI GUTE GROSCH" (16 Gute Groschen). A Gute Groschen (Good Groschen) is worth 1/24 of a thaler, where the lighter Marien Groschen (Mary's Groschen) is worth 1/36 of a thaler.

Below the value are the letters JJJ. These are the initials of the mint master Jobst Jakob Jänisch. He was mint master at Celle and Luneburg from 1687 - 1705.
